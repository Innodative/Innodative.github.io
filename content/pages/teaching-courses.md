## Graduate Courses

### Advanced Financial Accounting with AI (Spring 2025, 2024, 2023)

Modern financial accounting through the lens of artificial intelligence and machine learning. Topics include automated audit procedures, NLP for financial disclosure analysis, algorithmic bias in accounting applications, and blockchain for financial reporting.

**[Syllabus](example.pdf)** | **[Course Materials](/teaching-materials/)**

{% sidenote "sn-ai-accounting" "Developed with support from the AICPA Academic Excellence Award." %}

### Blockchain and Digital Assets (Fall 2024, 2023, 2022)

Distributed ledger technology, cryptocurrency accounting, smart contracts, and implications for financial reporting and assurance. Students build simple blockchain applications and analyze real crypto transactions.

**[Syllabus](example.pdf)** | **[GitHub Repository](https://github.com/example)**

### Data Analytics for Auditors (Spring 2024, 2023)

Statistical methods and machine learning for audit applications. Students work with real audit data (anonymized) to build predictive models for risk assessment and fraud detection.

**[Syllabus](example.pdf)**

### Seminar in Accounting Information Systems (Fall 2021, 2020)

PhD seminar covering research methods in AIS, emerging technologies, and current research frontiers.

**[Reading List](example.pdf)**

## Undergraduate Courses

### Data Science for Business (Fall 2024, 2023, 2022, 2021)

Introduction to statistical learning for business students with no programming background. Topics include regression, classification, clustering, and data visualization using Python.

**[Syllabus](example.pdf)** | **[Course Website](https://example.com)**{% marginnote "mn-data-sci" "Enrollment has grown from 45 to 180 students over four years." %}

### Financial Statement Analysis with Python (Spring 2023, 2022)

Hands-on course teaching students to analyze financial statements using Python. Covers data acquisition (APIs, web scraping), financial ratios, time series analysis, and basic prediction.

**[Syllabus](example.pdf)** | **[Jupyter Notebooks](https://github.com/example)**

### Introduction to Financial Accounting (Fall 2020, Spring 2021)

Standard introductory accounting course with emphasis on conceptual understanding and real-world applications.
