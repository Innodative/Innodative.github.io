{% newthought "My data science research" %} focuses on interpretable machine learning, transfer learning for structured data, and the development of foundation models for business applications. I'm particularly interested in methods that balance predictive performance with explainability.

---

## Refereed Papers

**Interpretable Neural Networks for Tabular Data**  
Smith, R., Anderson, M., & Brown, S. (2024). *Journal of Machine Learning Research*, 25, 1-34.  
[DOI](https://doi.org/example) | [Code](https://github.com/example){% sidenote "sn-jmlr" "Winner of the JMLR Best Paper Award, 2024." %}

Introduces a novel architecture that maintains the predictive power of deep learning while providing feature-level interpretability comparable to traditional methods.

**Transfer Learning in Financial Time Series**  
Smith, R., Davis, P., & Lee, Q. (2023). *Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS)*.  
[Paper](example.pdf) | [Poster](example.pdf) | [Code](https://github.com/example)

Demonstrates how pre-training on large-scale financial data improves few-shot learning for specialized forecasting tasks.

**Attention Mechanisms for Structured Business Data**  
Smith, R., Williams, T., & Garcia, M. (2023). *International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML)*.  
[Paper](example.pdf){% marginnote "mn-icml" "Oral presentation, acceptance rate 2.1%." %}

Adapts transformer architectures for tabular data, showing substantial improvements over traditional gradient boosting methods.

**Fairness-Aware Feature Selection**  
Smith, R. & Johnson, K. (2022). *AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence*.  
[Paper](example.pdf) | [Code](https://github.com/example)

Proposes methods for feature selection that explicitly account for fairness constraints across protected groups.

**Deep Learning for Anomaly Detection in Audit Data**  
Smith, R., Kumar, S., & Chen, W. (2022). *ACM SIGKDD International Conference on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining*.  
[Paper](example.pdf)

## Working Papers

**Foundation Models for Structured Business Data**  
Smith, R., Martinez, J., & Thompson, R. (2024). Under review.  
[SSRN](https://ssrn.com/example) | [Code](https://github.com/example)

Explores pre-training strategies for creating general-purpose models that can be fine-tuned for diverse business analytics tasks.

**Uncertainty Quantification in High-Stakes Predictions**  
Smith, R. & Anderson, P. (2024). Work in progress.

Develops methods for reliable uncertainty estimates in machine learning models used for critical business decisions.
