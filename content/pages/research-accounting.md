{% newthought "My accounting research" %} examines how artificial intelligence and emerging technologies are transforming financial reporting, audit procedures, and corporate disclosure practices. I work at the intersection of accounting, computer science, and information systems.

---

## Refereed Papers

**Algorithmic Bias in Automated Audit Procedures**  
Smith, R., Wilson, T., & Garcia, M. (2024). *Journal of Accounting Research*, 62(1), 89-124.  
[DOI](https://doi.org/example) | [SSRN](https://ssrn.com/example){% marginnote "mn-jar" "Featured in WSJ coverage of AI in accounting. Winner of JAR Best Paper Award." %}

Demonstrates that machine learning models used in audit procedures can perpetuate systematic biases, and proposes fairness-aware alternatives.

**Natural Language Processing of Earnings Call Transcripts**  
Smith, R. & Taylor, K. (2023). *The Accounting Review*, 98(5), 301-328.  
[DOI](https://doi.org/example) | [Replication Package](example.zip)

Shows that linguistic features extracted from earnings calls predict future firm performance beyond traditional financial metrics.

**Blockchain Technology and Audit Trail Integrity**  
Smith, R., Kumar, S., & Brown, D. (2023). *Contemporary Accounting Research*, 40(2), 867-901.  
[DOI](https://doi.org/example)

Evaluates the practical benefits and limitations of distributed ledger technology for creating tamper-resistant audit trails.

**Machine Learning in Fraud Detection**  
Smith, R., Anderson, P., & Lee, Q. (2022). *Journal of Accounting and Economics*, 73(2-3), 101450.  
[DOI](https://doi.org/example){% sidenote "sn-jae" "Among top 10 most-downloaded JAE articles in 2022." %}

Compares traditional fraud detection methods with modern machine learning approaches using real audit data.

**XBRL Adoption and Information Quality**  
Smith, R. & Chen, W. (2021). *Review of Accounting Studies*, 26(3), 1042-1081.  
[DOI](https://doi.org/example)

Examines how structured data formats affect financial statement comparability and analyst forecasts.
