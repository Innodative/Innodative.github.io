My astrophysics research focuses on stellar population synthesis, transient event classification, and computational methods for large astronomical surveys. Much of this work involves applying machine learning to time-domain astronomy.

Refereed Papers

Stellar Population Synthesis in Nearby Galaxies
Smith, R., Johnson, A., & Williams, K. (2023). The Astrophysical Journal, 912(2), 145-162.
DOI | PDF | Code

We present a novel approach to stellar population synthesis using Bayesian inference, allowing us to constrain star formation histories in nearby galaxies with unprecedented precision.

Machine Learning Classification of Transient Events
Smith, R., Peterson, L., Martinez, R., & Thompson, K. (2022). Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society, 501(3), 3421-3438.
DOI | arXiv

Developed a deep learning classifier achieving 96% accuracy in real-time classification of astronomical transients from survey data.

Photometric Redshifts Using Random Forests
Smith, R. & Anderson, M. (2021). Astronomy & Astrophysics, 645, A112.
DOI | PDF

Applied ensemble methods to photometric redshift estimation, demonstrating significant improvements over traditional template-fitting approaches.

Evolution of Massive Stars in Dense Environments
Smith, R., Chen, Q., & Davis, P. (2020). The Astrophysical Journal Letters, 891(1), L15.
DOI

Short-timescale observations reveal how stellar evolution differs in high-density cluster cores.
